# State of Clinical AI Report 2026

> Inaugural annual synthesis from the [ARISE Network](../entities/arise-network.md) (Stanford / Harvard / Beth Israel Deaconess collaboration). Released as a 130-slide deck (Google Slides) in January 2026. Each slide pairs a single 2024–2025 paper or topic with concise bullets and a citation, then closes each section with takeaways. Where the [AI Index 2026](ai-index-2026.md) Medicine chapter gave headline numbers, ARISE walks through the underlying primary literature — making this the closest thing the field has to a peer-reviewed-style ground truth for clinical AI.

## Summary

The report is organized into six sections — **Model Performance**, **Benchmarks & Evaluations**, **Foundational Methods**, **AI in Clinical Workflows**, **Patient-Facing AI**, and **Applied AI & Demos** — followed by a "10 Predictions for 2026" section. The leadership message frames the project bluntly: *"What actually holds up in practice?"* Frontier-model capability is no longer in dispute; what is contested is whether deployed systems actually improve patient outcomes, and where the safety floor lies.

The empirical picture ARISE constructs is **two-track and somewhat more skeptical than the AI Index Medicine chapter**. On the strong-evidence side: the first prospective LLM-copilot deployment in real care (Penda Health "AI Consult" in Kenya, 39,849 visits, 16% diagnostic-error reduction) ([ai-consult-penda](../entities/ai-consult-penda.md)); the largest real-world stroke-AI evaluation (Brainomix 360 across 26 NHS hospitals doubled EVT rates); the first two RCTs of ambient AI scribes (modest objective time savings ~20 s/note, real subjective burnout reduction); and prospective imaging RCTs in mammography ([Vara MG], 463k women, 17.6% higher cancer detection without higher recall) and ophthalmology ([EyeFM], 75 → 92% diagnostic accuracy with AI-assisted ophthalmologists). On the cautionary side: NOHARM ([noharm](../concepts/noharm.md)) shows top LLMs produce *severely harmful* advice in 10–20% of real consult cases, with **77% of severe harms being errors of omission** (failing to recommend critical tests or treatments); MetaMedQA shows 0% of LLMs recognize unanswerable questions; CRAFT-MD shows GPT-4 accuracy collapses 0.82 → 0.63 going from static vignettes to multi-turn dialogue; an "Optimization Paradox" study found a "best-of-breed" multi-agent ED system performed *worse* than less-optimized ones (68% vs 77%) because component-level wins broke information flow; colonoscopy ADR dropped 28.4% → 22.4% after AI exposure (deskilling, [ai-deskilling](../concepts/ai-deskilling.md)); and AI-trained physicians exhibited automation bias when fed deliberately erroneous LLM outputs (85% → 73% accuracy, [automation-bias-medical-ai](../concepts/automation-bias-medical-ai.md)).

ARISE's contribution to the wiki is therefore *primary citations for claims previously summarized via the AI Index*. The "5% used real clinical data" critique is now traceable to Bedi & Shah, JAMA Jan 2025, n=519 LLM eval studies 2022–2024. The o1-preview NEJM CPC numbers (78%/87%) and ED study (66% vs 48–54% physicians at triage) are now traceable to Brodeur et al. ArXiv Jul 2025. The MAI-DxO ~20%-physician comparison gets confirmed at 80% accuracy (paired with o3) and a 70% cost reduction (Nori, Horvitz et al. ArXiv Jun 2025, [mai-dxo](../entities/mai-dxo.md)). The report also identifies methodological themes the AI Index touched only briefly: **medical-event tokenization** as a foundation-model paradigm ([delphi-2m](../entities/delphi-2m.md), [comet](../entities/comet.md)), **multi-agent orchestration** with explicit failure modes (the optimization paradox), and **multimodal-readiness illusion** — strong benchmark scores often reflect shortcut learning rather than visual grounding (Gu, Vozila ArXiv Oct 2025).

## Key claims and findings

### Headline framing (slides 5–7)
- 1,200+ FDA-cleared AI tools and 350,000+ consumer health apps generate a $70B market. Of 691 FDA-cleared AI/ML medical devices (1995–2023), >95% went through 510(k); ~50% of device summaries omitted study design, 53% lacked sample size, <1% reported patient outcomes, 95% lacked demographic data, 91% lacked bias assessments. (See [fda-510k-pathway](../concepts/fda-510k-pathway.md) for primary citation chain.)
- Top takeaways: (1) capability accelerates, real-clinical-impact evidence lags; (2) frontier LLMs are uneven — strong on reasoning, brittle under uncertainty; (3) clinicians want admin/workflow automation, but those tasks are understudied; (4) patient-facing AI demands distinct safety guardrails; (5) multimodal clinical AI is approaching practical usability; (6) near-term FDA adoption favors narrow, task-specific systems.

### Model performance (slides 16–28)
- **o1-preview / o1 reach superhuman reasoning** on NEJM CPC (78% Dx, 87% next-test selection); 99% reasoning quality vs GPT-4 59% vs attendings 35%; 86% management reasoning vs 41–42% for physicians ± GPT-4. In real ED cases, o1-preview matched/beat attendings at three diagnostic touchpoints (66% exact-near-exact vs 48–54% physicians at triage). *Brodeur, Buckley, Manrai, Rodman et al., ArXiv, Jul 2025.* See [llm-clinical-reasoning](../concepts/llm-clinical-reasoning.md).
- **Google AMIE** (multi-agent, gemini-based) was non-inferior to 21 PCPs across 100 three-visit simulated scenarios; outperformed PCPs on medication-reasoning RxQA. *Palepu, Schaekermann et al., ArXiv, Mar 2025.* See [amie](../entities/amie.md).
- **Multimodal AMIE** outperformed PCPs on top-1 through top-10 differential across 105 multimodal OSCE cases (skin photos, ECGs, documents). *Saab, Freyberg, Tanno et al., ArXiv, May 2025.*
- **Dr. CaBot** (o3-based CPC discussant): 60% top-1, 84% top-10 on CPC differential, beat 20-physician baseline; physicians blindly rated its reasoning higher than human experts. *Buckley et al., ArXiv, Sept 2025.*
- **"None of the other answers" failure mode**: when the correct MedQA answer is replaced with NOTA, frontier models drop 9–38% in accuracy — strong MCQ performance is partly pattern matching, not reasoning. *Bedi, Shah et al., JAMA Network Open, Aug 2025.*
- **Script Concordance Testing (SCT)**: o3 led at 68%, GPT-4o 64% — matched students but below residents/attendings; LLMs overuse extreme ratings, rarely select neutrality. *McCoy, Rodman et al., NEJM AI, Sept 2025.*
- Prediction wins: wearable-data inpatient deterioration (AUROC 0.84–0.9, 8–24h before MEWS, *Scheid Nature Communications Jul 2025*); LLM biological age (c-index 0.76 all-cause mortality, beats epigenetic clocks, *Li Nature Medicine Jul 2025*); insulin resistance from wearables + labs (AUROC 0.80, *Metwally ArXiv Apr 2025*); JETS wearable foundation model (3M person-days, *Xie OpenReview Dec 2025*); AgentMD running 2,164 PubMed-sourced clinical calculators (88% vs GPT-4 41%, *Jin Nature Communications Oct 2025*).

### Benchmarks & evaluations (slides 29–39)
- **Bedi/Shah JAMA Jan 2025** is the primary source for the famous "5% used real clinical data" critique. n=519 LLM-eval studies 2022–2024: 95% used accuracy, only 5% used real patient data; 19% diagnoses, 0.2% billing, 0.2% prescriptions; 16% fairness/bias/toxicity, 5% deployment, 1% calibration. (Originally surfaced via [ai-index-2026](ai-index-2026.md) and [arise-network](../entities/arise-network.md).)
- [healthbench](../concepts/healthbench.md) (OpenAI): 5,000 conversations, 262 physicians from 60 countries, 48,562 rubric criteria. GPT-3.5 16% → o3 60%. *Arora, Singhal et al., ArXiv May 2025.*
- [medhelm](../concepts/medhelm.md): Stanford bundle of 35 benchmarks, 5 categories, 121 tasks — 12 use real EHR data. DeepSeek R1 (0.66) and o3-mini (0.64) led; admin/workflow lowest at 0.53–0.63. *Bedi, Shah et al., ArXiv Jun 2025.*
- [medagentbench](../concepts/medagentbench.md): virtual FHIR EHR with 100 patients, 700k+ data elements, 300 query+action tasks. Claude 3.5 Sonnet (70%), GPT-4o (64%) — strong on queries (85%), weak on actions (54%). *Jiang, Chen et al., NEJM AI Aug 2025.*
- [noharm](../concepts/noharm.md) (ARISE): 100 specialist consults, 4,249 management actions, 12,747 expert annotations. **Severe harm in up to 22% of cases across 31 LLMs; 77% of severe harms are errors of omission.** Three-agent "advisor + guardian" systems show ~6x higher odds of top-quartile safety. *Wu, Goh et al., ArXiv Dec 2025.*
- **MetaMedQA**: 12 LLMs underperform on metacognitive tasks; 0% recognize unanswerable questions. *Griot, Yuskel et al., Nature Communications Jan 2025.*
- **CRAFT-MD**: GPT-4 accuracy drops 0.82 → 0.63 going from static vignette to multi-turn conversation; 0.66 → 0.47 for GPT-3.5. *Johri, Rajpurkar et al., Nature Medicine Jan 2025.*

### Foundational methods (slides 40–58)
- **Medical-event tokenization** as a paradigm: Delphi-2M (UK Biobank, 400k participants → 1.9M Danish validation, AUC 0.76 for next-disease, simulates 20-year trajectories, *Shmatko Nature Sept 2025*, [delphi-2m](../entities/delphi-2m.md)); Epic CoMET (118M patients / 115B events, foundation model usable Feb 2026, *Waxler Shah ArXiv Nov 2025*, [comet](../entities/comet.md)).
- **Multi-agent orchestration**: [mai-dxo](../entities/mai-dxo.md) (80% w/ o3 vs 20% physicians, 70% cost cut); MAC framework (multi-agent rare-disease diagnosis, 78% vs GPT-4 58%, *Chen npj Digital Medicine Mar 2025*); TrialGenie (multi-agent trial design, *Lie medRxiv Apr 2025*).
- **The optimization paradox**: a "best-of-breed" multi-agent ED system (best component models) achieved 86% lab interpretation but only 68% diagnostic accuracy, *worse than* a less-optimized 77% multi-agent system. Agent compatibility and information flow matter more than component strength. *Bedi, Shah et al., ArXiv Jun 2025.*
- **Multimodal**: MUSK oncology FM (50M images, 1B text tokens, AUC 0.83 melanoma relapse, *Xiang Nature Jan 2025*); MOME breast MRI mixture-of-modality experts (AUROC 0.91 malignancy, *Luo Nature Communications Apr 2025*); EyeFM RCT (16 ophthalmologists, 668 patients, 75 → 92% diagnosis with AI, *Wu Nature Medicine Aug 2025*); kidney-cancer recurrence multimodal model (C-index 0.84 external validation, reclassified 83% of "low-risk" KEYNOTE-564, *Zang npj Digital Medicine Nov 2025*).
- **The illusion of readiness**: leading multimodal medical models score well even when key visual inputs are missing — shortcut learning, not grounded reasoning. *Gu, Vozila et al., ArXiv Oct 2025.*
- **Reasoning improvements**: Med-PRM process reward models (72–73%, *Yun ArXiv Sept 2025*); SourceCheckup audit of LLM citations — 50–90% of responses partly unsupported, GPT-4o + RAG only 55% response-level support (*Wu, Zou et al., Nature Communications Apr 2025*); fine-tuning frontier models for new medical knowledge generalizes weakly (drug facts 30%, EHRs 12%, guidelines 20% beyond memorized prompts), *Wu, Zou et al., NEJM AI Jul 2025.*

### AI in clinical workflows (slides 59–78)
- **GPT-4 management RCT**: physicians + GPT-4 ≈ GPT-4 alone (43% vs 44%) — suboptimal teaming. *Goh, Chen, Rodman et al., Nature Medicine Feb 2025.* See [llm-clinical-reasoning](../concepts/llm-clinical-reasoning.md).
- **Collaborative GPT-4 RCT** (n=70 physicians): custom collaborative interface lifted accuracy 75% → 82% (AI second opinion) → 85% (AI first opinion) vs 75% conventional. Did not exceed model alone. *Everett, Horvitz et al., medRxiv Jun 2025.*
- **AI Treatment Plans Outperform Doctors in Urgent Care** (461 telehealth encounters): AI optimal 77% vs 67%; potentially harmful 2.8% vs 4.6%. *Zeltzer, Pevnick et al., Annals of Internal Medicine Apr 2025.*
- **[ai-consult-penda](../entities/ai-consult-penda.md)** (Penda Health Kenya, 39,849 visits, 15 sites): 16% relative reduction in diagnostic errors, 13% in treatment errors. **First prospective LLM-copilot deployment in real care.** *Korom, Singhal et al., ArXiv Jul 2025.*
- **Vara MG mammography** (Germany, 12 sites, 463,094 women): BCDR 6.7 vs 5.7 per 1000 (17.6% increase), recall rate slightly *lower* (37.4 vs 38.3 per 1000). 204 cancers caught via "safety net" alerts; many DCIS. *Eisemann, Katalinic et al., Nature Medicine Jan 2025.* See [medical-imaging-ai](../concepts/medical-imaging-ai.md).
- **AI-enabled spirometry** (PCPs, 133 participants): improved top-diagnosis accuracy 9 percentage points, COPD identification 16 points, narrowing the PCP–pulmonologist gap. *Doe, Man et al., NEJM AI Jul 2025.*
- **Human-machine collaboration meta-analysis** (52 studies, 87 conditions): HMT > human-only on average, but rarely achieves full complementarity (1+1≠2); often does *not* beat the best of the two. Junior clinicians benefit more. *Liu, Chen et al., npj AI Dec 2025.*
- **Automation bias** in AI-trained physicians: deliberate-error vignettes dropped accuracy 85% → 73% despite voluntary AI consultation and prior AI-literacy training. *Qazi, Alizai et al., medRxiv Sept 2025.* See [automation-bias-medical-ai](../concepts/automation-bias-medical-ai.md).
- **Deskilling**: colonoscopy ADR fell 28.4% → 22.4% in 1,443 standard procedures *after* the same endoscopists had been exposed to AI polyp detection (OR 0.69 adjusted). *Budzyn, Mori et al., Lancet Gastroenterology & Hepatology Oct 2025.* See [ai-deskilling](../concepts/ai-deskilling.md).
- **First two RCTs of ambient AI scribes**: subjective burnout reductions but **objective time savings ~20 s/note** (DAX Copilot vs Nabla in Lukac/Mafi NEJM AI Nov 2025; Afshar/Gordon NEJM AI Nov 2025); related Olson/Troup *JAMA Network Open* Oct 2025 found burnout 52% → 39% (74% lower odds, n=272). See [ambient-ai-scribes](../concepts/ambient-ai-scribes.md).
- AI message routing: 469 patient-portal messages routed correctly 98% — time-to-first-interaction 1.2h → 0.2h; resolution 26.7h → 4.2h. *Anderson, Harzard et al., NEJM AI Feb 2025.*
- LLM discharge summaries match physician quality on Likert ratings (3.7 vs 3.8, more concise, more errors but low-harm). *Williams, Rosner et al., JAMA Internal Medicine May 2025.* VeriFact EHR-grounded fact-checking pipeline reaches 93% agreement with clinician consensus (*Chung, Aghaeepour et al., NEJM AI Dec 2025*).

### Patient-facing AI (slides 79–92)
- **AMIE diagnostic dialogue** matched/exceeded 20 PCPs on 30/32 specialist axes and 25/26 patient-actor axes across 159 simulated cases — empathy, accuracy, communication. *Tu, Natarajan et al., Nature Apr 2025.*
- **Overtrust risk**: lay participants couldn't distinguish AI (GPT-3) from physician answers; rated low-accuracy AI as equally trustworthy and would follow the advice. *Shekar, Maes et al., NEJM AI May 2025.*
- **AI-led DPP RCT** (n=368): noninferior to human-coach Diabetes Prevention Program on weight/HbA1c/activity composite (31.7% vs 31.9%); higher engagement, no adverse events. *Mathioudakis, Maruthur et al., JAMA Oct 2025.*
- **CV-Coach** (LLaMA 3-70B, fine-tuned on Transtheoretical Model): 68% of participants preferred AI messages when stage-matched, 85% in general comparisons; experts rated AI 4.4 vs 2.8 for effectiveness. *Mantena, Ashley et al., npj CV Health Sept 2025.*
- **GPTCoach** (motivational-interviewing wearable coach), **REVERIE** (VR sports system, 8-week RCT, fat-mass −4.3 kg vs −5 physical sports, plus working-memory and olfactory improvements; *Wang Nature Medicine Jun 2025*), **PH-LLM** (Gemini-fine-tuned sleep/fitness coach matching boards 79–88%, *Khasentino Nature Medicine Jul 2025*).
- **Plain-language translation of clinical notes** (GPT-4): improved objective comprehension by 1.2 of 4 points, with largest gains in Black, Hispanic, older, and low-health-literacy patients. *Kumar, Engelhard et al., NEJM AI Jan 2025.*
- **GPT-4o pediatric Spanish translation** statistically equivalent to professional translators; preferred 52% vs 20% by blinded evaluators. *Ray, Hron et al., JAMA Pediatrics Jul 2025.*

### Applied AI & demos (slides 93–125)
- **Imaging dominates**: celiac biopsy AI (>95% accuracy, indistinguishable agreement from pathologist–pathologist, *Jaeckle Soilleux NEJM AI Mar 2025*); inverted papilloma malignancy (AUC 99.8% on CT, *Hosseinzadeh Communications Medicine Oct 2025*); MOME breast MRI; **Brainomix 360 stroke** (England NHS, 26 hospitals, EVT 2.3 → 4.6% AI sites vs 1.6 → 2.6% non-AI, door-in-door-out 192 → 128 min, *Nagaratnam Lancet Dec 2025*); LCTfound lung CT (105k scans, AUROC 0.95 rare disease); Woollie oncology LLM (AUROC up to 0.97 progression on radiology reports); UltraBot autonomous carotid US robot (>90% scan success, 5.5× human reproducibility); Adnan smile-video Parkinson's (80–85% accuracy, *NEJM AI Jun 2025*); DeepRETStroke retinal stroke prediction; DeepDKD diabetic-kidney from retinal images (>734k images, AUC 0.79–0.84).
- **ECG models**: [echonext](../entities/echonext.md) detects structural heart disease from ECGs (1.2M ECG-echo pairs, 77% vs cardiologist 64%); [present-shd](../entities/present-shd.md) extends to phone-photographed ECGs (AUROC 0.85–0.90); ROMIAE rules out AMI in ED with NPV 99.1%; cirrhosis-from-ECG ML model doubled new advanced-liver-disease diagnoses in primary care (*Simonetto, Shah et al., Nature Medicine Dec 2025*).
- **Critical care / surgery**: RL vasopressin policy in septic shock (n=10,217, concordant care had 19% lower mortality, *Kalimouttou Pirracchio JAMA Mar 2025*); colorectal-surgery AI risk stratification (n=18,403 → prospective bundles cut severe complications 28% → 19%, readmissions IRR 0.66, cost-saving in 97% of scenarios, *Rosen Gogenur Nature Medicine Sept 2025*); Smart Match transfusion ML (24,003 surgeries, AUROC 0.94, *Bishara Eng NEJM AI Nov 2025*).
- **Operational**: ED triage / diagnosis with Claude 3.5 Sonnet + RAG (66% triage accuracy on 2,000 MIMIC-IV cases); referral-gatekeeping AI (45,039 referrals, +19% accuracy, 5 specialties); Auto-MACE adjudicates cardiovascular-trial endpoints (97% CV death, 89% MI, 88% stroke agreement, preserves trial conclusions in PARADISE-MI); RECTIFIER trial prescreening (eligibility 15 vs 50 days, enrollment 2% vs 1%, *Unlu Blood JAMA Feb 2025*); HopeLLM oncology trial matching at City of Hope (88% recall@10).
- **Demos / leaderboards**: ChatEHR (Stanford HIPAA-compliant EHR LLM); SAGE (specialist-AI eConsult, Stanford); Chopper (on-prem EHR LLM, Belgium, 50k+ conversations); MAST + NOHARM leaderboard (`bench.arise-ai.org`); Grow Therapy mental-health between-session AI coach.

### 10 predictions for 2026 (slide 127)
1. First malpractice lawsuit where AI plays a significant part in error.
2. AI agents for urgent care will hit mainstream across health systems.
3. Labeling systems for clinical data will be integrated into actual workflows.
4. Health systems and insurers will lock in an "AI-bot arms race" over prior auth, claims denials, and coding optimization. *No net benefit to anyone except vendors.*
5. FDA will explore different regulatory mechanisms for generative AI, but no significant progress.
6. More people will receive medical therapy / counseling / advice from AI than from live humans.
7. >90% of clinical-note text will be AI-generated (ambient scribe). No one is sure what the clinician actually thought.
8. Prospective clinical-AI CDS deployment results will start arriving.
9. Frontier models will keep beating humans on benchmarks; new research will focus on human-machine collaboration.
10. Continued growth in scribe market; capabilities will expand to downstream workflow tasks.

## Notes

- **Format**: 130-slide Google Slides deck, exported as PDF. Each slide ≈ one paper. Citations are inline; the deck has no formal bibliography section, so each wiki claim is best traced via the slide number and the inline first-author/journal reference.
- **Independence claim**: "ARISE's research program is supported through grants and industry research partners. This report was prepared independently." Disclosures (slide 128) note Goh consults for Google, OpenAI, Samsung Research America, Roche Diagnostics, Novartis, Hello Heart, Grow Care Inc, and Faculty Connection; Rodman receives funding from Moore Foundation, Macy Foundation, NIH, ARPA-H, Google, and Google DeepMind; Chen has multiple conflicts including paid consulting and Reaction Explorer cofounder. Several "demo" slides flag sponsorship (Grow Therapy explicitly marked as "Research partner demo," SAGE/Grow Therapy listed under "sponsored").
- **Methodological caveat from ARISE itself**: "Excluded purely technical model papers without clinician- or patient-facing evaluation, editorials, and non-clinical AI (e.g., drug discovery, biotech)." So this is *clinical AI* in the deployment-focused sense; it does not cover biomedical foundation models that don't touch clinical workflows ([alphafold-3](../entities/alphafold-3.md), [evo-2](../entities/evo-2.md), etc., remain in the AI Index Science chapter).
- **Citation precedence**: where the same finding is in both [ai-index-2026](ai-index-2026.md) and ARISE, **prefer ARISE as primary** — it cites the underlying paper directly and per-study, while AI Index aggregates.
- **Open question raised**: ARISE explicitly contradicts the "fundamental theorem of medical informatics" (human + AI > human or AI alone) — Liu Chen npj AI Dec 2025 and the Goh GPT-4 RCT both show teaming often does *not* beat AI alone. This deserves its own concept page eventually if the literature continues to mount.
- **Adjacent sources to ingest next**: the underlying primary papers, especially Brodeur ED (ArXiv 2025), MAI-DxO (`arxiv.org/abs/2506.22405` — second source in this batch), Bedi/Shah JAMA Jan 2025, NOHARM (Wu/Goh ArXiv Dec 2025).
- **Stale claim flagged**: existing wiki entries citing AI Index 2026 for the "5%" stat should be cross-referenced with this Bedi/Shah JAMA Jan 2025 primary citation now that we have it.
